Event Of The Day

GGAI held a public lecture on “People Governance Policy” on 5 November 2025. The focus was on “strengthening governance systems, accountability and policy engagement.”

The event featured Dr. Bong Duke, Vice Chairman and CEO of the Cross River State Planning Commission as the guest lecturer. Attendees included government officials, civil society members, media representatives and development partners.

The lecture emphasized the importance of inclusive governance, transparency and citizen participation in policy-making processes. Dr. Duke highlighted strategies for enhancing governance structures and fostering accountability at all levels of government.

The featured speaker; Dr. Bong Duke, who made a call for handing over federal roads to states to improve “real accountability.” There was a panel discussion and interactive session with delegates from civil society. The lecture was covered on social media (Facebook, Instagram) and news platforms. The event also had a celebratory angle: ” GGAI’s DG, Sir Leonard Anyogo’s “Golden Age.”

In related media, GGAI (through its DG) defended a 15% petroleum import tariff, saying it would stabilize the naira and attract investors. The lecture message reinforced GGAI’s wider mission: promoting people-centered governance, accountability and stronger policy engagement.

Our Guest Lecturer Of The Day

Dr. Bong Duke

Dr. Bong Duke is the Vice Chairman and CEO of the Cross River State Planning Commission in Nigeria. He has over 25 years of experience in strategic planning and international consultancy. He studied Strategic Planning in South Korea, holds a postgraduate degree in Economics from the University of Calabar, and even earned a Doctorate in Divinity from the Zoe Life Institute in the US. He has also had roles like CEO of Donald Duke Vendors and special assistant to a former governor on strategic planning.

Dr. Duke has worked with various international organizations, including the World Bank and UNDP, focusing on development projects in Africa. He is known for his expertise in governance, economic development and public policy.
